Welcome to Part I. To effectively adapt and deploy Large Language Models, we first need a deep understanding of the architectural foundations. In this section, we will examine the three primary Transformer architectures—encoder-only, decoder-only, and encoder-decoder—and analyze their relative strengths. We'll then move into the mechanics of attention, exploring how multi-head and FlashAttention enable efficiency at scale. We will also cover scaling laws and the emergence of Mixture of Experts (MoE) for sparse activation, concluding with the core objectives and computational costs of base model training. This theoretical grounding is essential before we move into data engineering and fine-tuning.
